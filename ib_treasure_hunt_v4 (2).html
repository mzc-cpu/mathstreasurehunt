Subtract: \(a = -8\);  then \(d = 4\).
Answers: \(a = -8\), \(d = 4\)
🔮 Where your future paths unravelQuestion 2 — Differentiation & Normal
(a) \(y=(x-1)^{-1}\)  →  \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = -(x-1)^{-2} = \dfrac{-1}{(x-1)^2}\).
(b) At \(x=3\): \(y=\tfrac{1}{2}\), gradient of tangent \(= -\tfrac{1}{4}\), so normal gradient \(= 4\).
   \(y - \tfrac{1}{2} = 4(x-3)\)
Answers: (a) \(\dfrac{-1}{(x-1)^2}\)   (b) \(y = 4x - \dfrac{23}{2}\)
☕ Where we steal the kettle fromQuestion 3 — Trig Equation
\(\sin2x - 2\cos^2x = 2\sin x\cos x - 2\cos^2 x = 2\cos x(\sin x - \cos x)=0\).
\(\cos x=0 \Rightarrow x=\tfrac{\pi}{2},\tfrac{3\pi}{2}\);  \(\tan x=1 \Rightarrow x=\tfrac{\pi}{4},\tfrac{5\pi}{4}\).
Answers: \(x = \dfrac{\pi}{4},\ \dfrac{\pi}{2},\ \dfrac{5\pi}{4},\ \dfrac{3\pi}{2}\)
🎨 Where paints get mixed with treble clefsQuestion 4 — Substitution
\(u=\ln x,\ du=\tfrac{1}{x}dx\). Limits: \(x=e\Rightarrow u=1\); \(x=e^2\Rightarrow u=2\).
\(\displaystyle\int_1^2 \frac{1}{u}\,du = \big[\ln u\big]_1^2 = \ln 2 - 0\).
Answer: \(\ln 2\)
🌿 Where you come to do maths but end up chatting for an hour eating pearsQuestion 5 — Area & Volume (y = x cos x)
Region from \(x=0\) to \(x=\tfrac{\pi}{2}\). IBP for area: \(\int_0^{\pi/2}x\cos x\,dx = \big[x\sin x + \cos x\big]_0^{\pi/2} = \tfrac{\pi}{2}-1\).
Volume \(= \pi\int_0^{\pi/2}x^2\cos^2 x\,dx\). Using \(\cos^2x=\tfrac{1+\cos 2x}{2}\) and IBP twice gives \(\dfrac{\pi^2(\pi^2-6)}{48}\).
Answers: (a) \(\dfrac{\pi}{2}-1\)   (b) \(\dfrac{\pi^2(\pi^2-6)}{48}\)
➕ Maths Room ★Question 6 — Cylinder Optimisation & Related Rates
(a) \(h=\tfrac{500}{\pi r^2}\), so \(A=2\pi r^2+\tfrac{1000}{r}\).
(b) \(\tfrac{dA}{dr}=4\pi r-\tfrac{1000}{r^2}=0 \Rightarrow r^3=\tfrac{250}{\pi} \Rightarrow r=\sqrt[3]{\tfrac{250}{\pi}}\).
(c) \(\tfrac{dV}{dt}=\pi\!\left(2rh\tfrac{dr}{dt}+r^2\tfrac{dh}{dt}\right)=\pi(2\cdot5\cdot6\cdot(-3)+25\cdot2)=\pi(-180+50)=-130\pi\) cm³ s⁻¹.
Answers: (a) \(A=2\pi r^2+\dfrac{1000}{r}\)   (b) \(r=\sqrt[3]{\dfrac{250}{\pi}}\)   (c) \(-130\pi\ \text{cm}^3\text{s}^{-1}\)
🏛 The main gate to the realm of IBQuestion 7 — Binomial Expansion
General term: \(\binom{7}{r}\!\left(\tfrac{2}{x^3}\right)^{7-r}\!\!\left(-\tfrac{x^4}{3}\right)^r\). Power of \(x\): \(4r-3(7-r)=7r-21=0 \Rightarrow r=3\).
Term \(= \binom{7}{3}\cdot 2^4\cdot\dfrac{(-1)^3}{3^3} = 35\cdot16\cdot\dfrac{-1}{27}\).
Answer: \(-\dfrac{560}{27}\)

(b) \(V=\pi\!\int_0^{\pi/2}\!\sin^2(2x)\,dx\). Use \(\sin^2(2x)=\tfrac{1}{2}-\tfrac{1}{2}\cos4x\):
\(V=\pi\!\left[\tfrac{x}{2}-\tfrac{\sin4x}{8}\right]_0^{\pi/2}=\pi\!\left(\tfrac{\pi}{4}-0\right)\).

📖 The staircase to heaven, built on the dreams and words of the great mindsQuestion 9 — Implicit Differentiation
(a) Differentiate \(xy^2=y-2x^2+2\sqrt{x}\) implicitly: \(y^2+2xy\tfrac{dy}{dx}=\tfrac{dy}{dx}-4x+\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\). Rearrange to obtain given result. ✓
(b) At \(x=1\): \(y^2-y=0\Rightarrow y=0\) or \(y=1\). So \(p=0,\ q=1\).
(c) At P(1,0): gradient \(=\tfrac{0-4+1}{0-1}=3\); tangent \(y=3x-3\).
   At Q(1,1): gradient \(=\tfrac{-1-4+1}{2-1}=-4\); tangent \(y=-4x+5\).
   Intersect: \(3x-3=-4x+5\Rightarrow x=\tfrac{8}{7},\ y=\tfrac{3}{7}\).
Answers: (b) \(p=0,\ q=1\)   (c) \(T=\!\left(\tfrac{8}{7},\tfrac{3}{7}\right)\)
